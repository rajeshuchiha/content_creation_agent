    <h1>G4D Open: A New Era at Celtic Manor</h1>
    <p>Exciting news for the world of golf! The prestigious G4D Open is set to establish a new home in Wales, with the magnificent Celtic Manor Resort agreeing to host the championship for a three-year period. This significant announcement, published on 17 September 2025, marks a thrilling new chapter for disabled golf, bringing the globe's finest golfers with disabilities to a renowned venue.</p>

    <h2>A New Home for Elite Disabled Golf</h2>
    <p>The Celtic Manor Resort in Newport will stage the G4D Open in 2026, 2027, and 2028. This will be the first time the championship is played in Wales, offering a fantastic opportunity for both players and fans in the region to experience top-tier golf. The event will take place on the Roman Road course, a challenging and picturesque layout, rather than the Twenty Ten course, which famously hosted the Ryder Cup 15 years ago.</p>

    <h2>Championship Details and Legacy</h2>
    <p>First played in 2023, the G4D Open is a collaborative effort, jointly run by The R&A and the DP World Tour. It receives vital support from the European Disabled Golf Association (EDGA), highlighting a strong commitment to promoting golf for individuals with disabilities. The championship attracts a diverse field of 80 male and female amateur and professional golfers, all competing at the highest level.</p>
    <p>Competitors will face a rigorous challenge over 54 holes of stroke play gross competition. The tournament embraces inclusivity by featuring nine distinct sport classes, accommodating multiple impairment groups. This structure ensures fair and competitive play, celebrating the skill and determination of all participants.</p>

    <h2>Previous Champions and Future Stars</h2>
    <p>Looking back at this year's event, Brendan Lawlor and Daphne van Houten emerged as the respective men's and women's champions. Their success underscores the incredible talent present within disabled golf, and anticipation is already building to see who will claim victory at Celtic Manor in the coming years.</p>
    <p>The G4D Open at Celtic Manor promises to be a highlight on the international golf calendar, further elevating the profile of disabled golf and inspiring a new generation of players. It's an event not to be missed!</p>
